How it started

From a college
project to a real platform.

What started as a third-year engineering project turned into something we genuinely cared about. We were tired of missing events at our own college because nobody knew about them.

So we built Eventaura — a platform where any organiser can list an event in minutes, and any student or professional can discover what's happening around them in real time.

Today we're a tight team of three, shipping fast, learning faster, and obsessed with the product we're building.

2024 · Q3

💡 The Idea

Frustrated by missed events and scattered WhatsApp forwards, we decided to build a better way.

2024 · Q4

🔨 First Lines of Code

Anuj kicked off the Spring Boot backend. Ashutosh designed the database schema. Bhanu started brand research.

2025 · Q1

🎨 Design Overhaul

The UI went through three complete rewrites until it felt truly premium. Dark mode, live SSE updates, and mobile-first.

2025 · Q2

🚀 First Public Launch

We opened to our campus first — 200 registrations in the first 48 hours. The feedback was electric.

2026 · Now

🌍 Scaling Across India

Expanding to 50+ cities, onboarding clubs and tech communities, and continuously shipping new features.

What drives us

Our Core Values

The principles that guide every decision we make — big or small.

⚡

Ship Fast, Learn Faster

We believe in launching early and iterating based on real feedback. Perfect is the enemy of shipped.

🎯

User Obsession

Every feature we build starts with one question: does this make the experience better for the person using it?

🔓

Radical Openness

We believe events should be accessible to everyone — no paywalls, no hidden fees, no friction.

🛠️

Craftsmanship

We care deeply about the details — the animation curve, the loading state, the error message. Everything is intentional.

🤝

Community First

We're building this for communities — college clubs, tech groups, local organisers — not just enterprises.

📈

Data-Driven Growth

Every decision is backed by data. We measure what matters, drop what doesn't, and double down on what works.
